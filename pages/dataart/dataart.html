    In total, this number is an absurdly low one, when we compare it to other sub-genre's, like shooters, open-worlds and RPGs, which have seen a very noticable uptic in their development. There are some underlying factors to this however, one of them being stealh games, requiring very complex and inovative systems for almost every single new iteration within the genre. Metal Gear Solid V for example, is one of the most well rounded action-adventure game of any in terms of it's mechanic complexity and intuitivness.

    This opens up an even Bigger issue however, one that isn't just limited to a single genre, but is affecting the entirety of the games industry. One that is caused by exploding budgets, and the avoidance in any of failure that comes with it. Devs/Publishers are less in line with making expensive big blockbuster games, with the advent failure of Concord, Skull n Bones and so on serving as warnings.
