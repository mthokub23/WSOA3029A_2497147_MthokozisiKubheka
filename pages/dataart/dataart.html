    Unironically, while games are getting bigger, they're also getting less riskier. Studios and publishers are still making huge, AAA blockbuster games, they've become less complex in their mechanics, storytelling approach and visuals. They towards the already established series mechanics, more common storytelling and strive for "realism in visuals" and fidelity over stylistic more memorable artstyle. The stealth sub genre unfortunately is one that often requires levels of creativity to keep the mechanics innovative and intuitive.

    The end of Snake and Sam Fisher ? Not quite however. With the upcoming remasters/remakes of Metal Gear Solid 3 and Splinter Cell, there is still hope. However, only remakes and no true new IPs that push the genre forward. Our heroes might have seen their last days in the teenies. Our only hero left, is Agent 47. Thank You
