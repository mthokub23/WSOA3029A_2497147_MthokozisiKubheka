    User Interface Elements

    Navigation: Buttons, scrollbars, and links

    Navigation elements play a crucial role in guiding users through your website. These include:

    • Buttons: Used for interaction, such as the "back to top" button and toggles for dark mode.
    • Scrollbars: Enable users to move through the content. We’ve used a standard scrollbar for content overflow.
    • Links: Internal navigation links help users jump between sections or articles, such as the links in the navigation bar.

    Media: Images and Videos

    Media elements enhance the visual appeal and provide additional information to users:

    • Images: Used to represent featured content like game covers, UI components, or illustrations, e.g., in the gaming news section.
    • Videos: Embedded video clips to showcase gameplay, trailers, or tutorials could be added in the future to provide a richer user experience.

    Data: Tables and Charts

    We use data visualization to display key information dynamically:

    • Tables: For displaying game information, such as the top games list, complete with release dates, platforms, and Metacritic scores.
    • Charts: Data visualizations made with D3.js for visual storytelling (e.g., trends in stealth game releases).

    Forms: Interactive elements, such as search bars, could be implemented for searching through games or filtering results in the future.
